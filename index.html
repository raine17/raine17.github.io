JOUR 407/807

Crete's growing Latino population

By Emily Case

Josie Filipi had the typical Nebraskan dream: to start a family farm in the countryside. Filipi, who grew up in Mexico, moved to Bruning in 1985. Her family now lives on a farm in Saline County and she works in Crete.

When Filipi and her family first moved to Bruning, they were the only Mexican family in the community. Now they are part of a rapidly growing Latino population in the nearby Crete area. The city’s population skyrocketed 39.46 percent from 1990-2015. In comparison, other Nebraska cities of similar size have seen far less growth during this time than Crete.

US Census Bureau

Crete’s growth is part of a statewide trend of growing Latino populations. While the Asian population is growing at a faster rate, the Latino populace is by far the largest minority group in Nebraska. The percentage of Latinos jumped from 2.3 percent of the overall population in 1990 to 10.4 in 2015. And the Latino population could triple by 2050, according to the University of Nebraska at Omaha’s Center for Public Affairs Research (CPAR).

US Census Bureau and UNO CPAR

Crete’s population growth is also reflected in its school district. A new high school opened this year to accommodate the growing district size. In addition, Latino populations have outnumbered white populations in the school district since the 2010-2011 school year.

Nebraska Department of Education

While Crete has had a helpful network of minority-focused organizations, the City of Crete also hired Dulce Castañeda as community assistance director in June 2016. Her responsibilities revolve around helping newcomers and minority populations. This is considered to be the first government position of its kind in the state of Nebraska, according to City of Crete officials.

In a meeting with Economic Development Director Shawnna Silvius, Dulce Castañeda translates information about developing a small business for Crete community member and aspiring entrepreneur Reynold Nuñez. Castañeda helped 146 people in her first three months as Community Assistance Director.

The new position has been viewed as positive by many in the community, including Filipi. She has been a parent educator and home visitor at Blue River Family Resource Center, an umbrella organization that provides education and resources to families, for 18 years. Having a community assistance director makes her job easier and improves the lives of families living in Crete.

"We have a collaboration with United Church of Christ and with the City of Crete," she said. "[Newcomers] just tell us what they need and we can make the phone calls, and now it's even better because we just call Dulce."
